Prawns Devel

  • Notes
  • Video

Prawns Devel is a vibrant and spicy Sri Lankan dish featuring succulent shrimp that is marinated and stir-fried with onions, bell peppers, and a medley of bold spices. The shrimp is cooked to perfection, allowing the flavors to meld and create a dish that's both fiery and tangy. A touch of vinegar or lemon juice adds a zesty twist, enhancing the overall taste. Shrimp Devel is known for its intense flavors and is often served with rice or enjoyed as a tantalizing appetizer. It's a favorite in Sri Lankan cuisine for those who appreciate a spicy seafood dish with a punch.

Ingredients

To Marinate and Fry Shrimp

  • 500 g Shrimp (cleaned and deveined)
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Ground black pepper
  • 2 tsp Lime juice
  • 2 tbsp Avocado/canola/coconut/vegetable oil

To Make the Sauce

  • 1/2 tsp Cornstarch
  • 3 tbsp Cold or room temperature water
  • 3 tbsp Ketchup
  • 1 tsp Light or regular soy sauce
  • 1/4 tsp Sugar

Other Stir-Fry Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp Avocado/canola/vegetable/coconut oil if needed
  • 6 cloves Garlic, chopped
  • 1-inch piece of Ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp Chili flakes
  • 1 tbsp Sri Lankan-Chinese chili paste (check the description for substitutes)
  • 1/2 of a Red onion, cut into large chunks
  • 1 large Banana pepper, cut into thick slices (use more if you like more heat)
  • 1 Tomato, cut into wedges

How to Make Shrimp Devel Step by Step

  1. Marinate shrimp with salt, black pepper, and lime juice. Set it aside for 15 minutes.
  2. Mix water and cornstarch together in a small bowl. Add ketchup, soy sauce, and sugar to it and mix well to combine. Set aside.
  3. Heat a wok or large sauté pan to high heat. Add oil and let it heat up. Add shrimp to the pan and fry on high heat until shrimp are completely cooked (no raw parts visible).
  4. Turn off the heat. Using a slotted spoon, drain and remove shrimp from the pan to a separate clean plate. (If there's any juice left in the pan, turn the heat back on and let the liquid evaporate.)
  5. Turn the heat to medium. Add another 1 tbsp of oil (you can skip adding extra oil if your pan already has enough oil left from frying shrimp). Add ginger and garlic. Sauté until garlic turns light brown on the edges.
  6. Then add chili flakes and Sri Lankan-Chinese chili paste. Sauté for a few seconds.
  7. Add shrimp, onions, and peppers to the pan and mix until all the shrimp are coated with chili flakes.
  8. Lower the heat. Add the sauce. Mix well to coat everything with the sauce.
  9. Finally, add tomatoes. Gently mix with shrimp.

Notes:

This recipe yields a dish that typically serves 2-3 people.
The portion size may vary depending on whether it is served as a main dish or alongside other items.
