What is Kottu Roti?

This dish has become the national food of Sri Lanka (hypothetically). It can be found in every corner of Sri Lanka and every food shop makes kottu. Originally a street food, now even five-star hotels feature it on their menus due to its popularity.

About Sri Lankan Kottu Roti

If you ever visit Sri Lanka, no trip is complete without eating this amazing spicy dish. It’s designed to be eaten standing up, as it was meant to be a one-pot meal. The distinctive sound of street vendors making kottu is hard to miss. The real name is Kottu Roti, but it’s commonly referred to as ‘kottu’ or ‘koththu’.

Can I Make Kottu Roti at Home?

Yes, it’s quite simple. Many high-end restaurants prepare kottu roti by having the roti shredded and the condiments ready. They then cook it in a wok or pan, which is the method used in my recipe.

Can I Substitute the Roti?

You can use store-bought paratha or roti, but making it from scratch is easy and provides better flavor. Click here to get my roti recipe.

While Sri Lankan households often buy roti instead of making it, being abroad might require you to make it at home to satisfy cravings.
