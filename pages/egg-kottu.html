Egg Kottu

Egg Kottu is a delicious vegetarian variation of the traditional Sri Lankan Kottu Roti, made by stir-frying shredded roti with eggs and vegetables. It's a quick and flavorful dish that's perfect for a hearty meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 large roti or paratha, shredded
  • 3 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 green chili, sliced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, minced
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oil (vegetable or coconut)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (optional)

How to Make Egg Kottu Step by Step

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add the sliced onion, carrot, green chili, and tomato to the pan. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened. Season with salt, pepper, curry powder, turmeric, and cumin.
  3. Push the vegetables to one side of the pan and add the beaten eggs. Scramble the eggs until fully cooked, then mix them with the vegetables.
  4. Add the shredded roti to the pan along with the soy sauce. Stir everything together until the roti is evenly mixed with the eggs and vegetables. Continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Taste and adjust the seasoning with more salt, pepper, or soy sauce if needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with lime wedges on the side.

Notes:

This Egg Kottu recipe typically serves 2-3 people.
You can adjust the spiciness by varying the amount of chili used. Add more vegetables or include other ingredients like cheese or tofu for a unique variation.
