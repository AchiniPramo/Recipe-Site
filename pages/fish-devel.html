Fish Devel

Fish Devel is a vibrant and spicy Sri Lankan dish featuring tender pieces of fish that are marinated and stir-fried with onions, bell peppers, and a rich blend of bold spices. The fish is cooked to perfection, absorbing the deep flavors of the spices to create a dish that's both fiery and tangy. A hint of vinegar or lemon juice adds a zesty twist, elevating the overall taste. Fish Devel is known for its intense flavors and is often served with rice or enjoyed as a tantalizing appetizer. It's a beloved dish in Sri Lankan cuisine, perfect for those who enjoy a spicy seafood experience with a punch.

Ingredients

To Marinate Fish

  • 500 g Tuna Fish (500g = 1.1lb)
  • 2 tsp paprika or chili powder (use more if you like it hot)
  • 2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp water

For the Sauce

  • 2 tsp corn starch
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 1 tsp light or regular soy sauce
  • 2 tsp light or regular soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Other

  • 1 tbsp coconut, avocado or canola oil (for stir-frying)
  • 1 piece of cinnamon (preferably true Ceylon cinnamon)
  • 6 cloves of garlic
  • 1 inch piece of ginger
  • 1 sprig of curry leaves
  • 1/2 of a small pandan leaf broken into pieces
  • 1 1/2 tbsp chili flakes (reduce for less heat)
  • 2 tsp Sri Lankan-Chinese chili paste
  • 1 tsp black pepper, freshly ground
  • 1 large banana pepper
  • 1/4 of a red onion
  • 1 tomato
  • Enough oil for deep frying fish
  • Salt to your taste

How to Make the Fish Devel Step by Step

Marinate and Fry Fish

  1. Cut tuna into large chunks (about 2 inches long cuboids).
  2. Make the marinade with paprika or chili powder, black pepper, lime juice, salt, and water. Add the fish pieces and mix to coat every piece with the marinade. Be careful to do it gently as the fish pieces can break apart. Set it aside and let it marinate until you make the sauce and get the other ingredients ready (about 10 - 15 mins).


  3. Heat enough oil to deep fry in a deep skillet. Wait until the oil is hot. Gently add fish pieces to the oil and fry them until the edges start to turn golden and the fish is cooked through. Transfer fish to a paper towel-lined plate.

Make the Sauce

  1. Add cornstarch and water to a small bowl. Mix. Then add all the remaining sauce ingredients: ketchup, chili sauce, soy sauce, sugar, and salt. Mix and set aside.

Making the Devilled Fish

  1. Crush ginger and garlic in a mortar and pestle or chop them very finely.


  2. Cut banana peppers into rounds. Cut onion into large triangular pieces or rounds, and tomato into wedges.


  3. Heat a medium-sized wok or a saute pan. Add oil and let it heat up. Add cinnamon stick, ginger, garlic, curry leaves, and pandan leaves. Saute until the garlic starts to turn golden on the edges.


  4. Add chili flakes, chili paste, and black pepper. Saute for about 10 seconds or until chili flakes turn dark red.


  5. Add banana peppers and onions. Saute for another 10-15 seconds to mix through.


  6. Move everything to the side and add the sauce. Let the sauce heat up. When the sauce is heated, mix it with the vegetables.


  7. Add fried fish pieces. Gently mix everything well to coat all the fish pieces with the sauce (be careful not to break the fish pieces). Adjust salt if needed.


  8. Add tomatoes. Mix very lightly. You can serve the dish at this point or proceed to the next step if you prefer more gravy.


  9. Add 1/2 cup of water and mix. Let the gravy heat up, and when it starts to thicken, turn off the heat. It will thicken further as it cools down. Avoid simmering too long to prevent overcooking the fish and vegetables.

Notes:

This recipe yields a dish that typically serves 2-3 people. The portion size may vary depending on whether it is served as a main dish or alongside other items.
