Chicken Kottu

Chicken Kottu is a popular Sri Lankan street food made by stir-frying shredded roti with vegetables, eggs, and chicken. This dish is known for its unique blend of spices, vibrant flavors, and the characteristic sound of its preparation on the streets of Sri Lanka.

Ingredients

  • 2 large roti or paratha, shredded
  • 200g chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 green chili, sliced
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, minced
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oil (vegetable or coconut)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Lime wedges (optional)

How to Make Chicken Kottu Step by Step

  1. Heat oil in a wok and add minced ginger, garlic, and leek rings (white part or light green part only).
  2. Add the chicken pieces you took from the chicken curry to the wok. Break them using your wok spatula. You can also cut them beforehand if it's hard to break the chicken pieces with the spatula.
  3. Mix with the rest of the ingredients and let the chicken get brown a little bit.
  4. Move everything to a side and crack an egg to the middle of the wok. Let it fry for a few seconds and then scramble the egg. When the egg is almost cooked, mix with the rest of the ingredients on the wok.
  5. Move the mixture aside and add your chopped onion and sliced chilies. You can substitute chilies with chili flakes or you can use both fresh chilies and chili flakes for some extra heat.
  6. Mix everything well together. Keep mixing.

    Again, move everything aside and add the julienned carrots. Mix only the carrots to fry them a little bit.

    Mix everything together and add your diced tomatoes.
  7. Mix on high heat for about a minute.

    Add the chicken curry. You can add more or less according to the texture you want. If you like a dry kottu you can add less.
  8. Turn the heat to low. Mix all the roti pieces with the curry. Break the roti pieces and vegetables with the wok spatula while mixing.

Notes:

This Chicken Kottu recipe typically serves 2-3 people.
You can adjust the spiciness by varying the amount of chili used. Feel free to add more vegetables or substitute the chicken with another protein, such as beef or tofu, for a different variation.
