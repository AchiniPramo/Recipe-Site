Taco Pizza

Taco Pizza is an exciting twist on the traditional pizza, bringing together the bold and zesty flavors of a taco with the crispy, cheesy base of a pizza. This delicious creation is topped with seasoned ground beef, melted cheese, fresh lettuce, diced tomatoes, and a sprinkle of crunchy tortilla chips. Finished with a drizzle of tangy taco sauce and a dollop of sour cream, Taco Pizza offers a fun and flavorful experience that's sure to satisfy both pizza and taco lovers alike.

Ingredients

Pizza Dough

  • 2 tsp Active dry yeast
  • 4 1/2 cups 00 Flour
  • 2 tsp Kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp Extra virgin olive oil
  • All-purpose flour for dusting
  • 1 3/4 cups Water

Taco Seasoning

  • 2 tsp Chili powder
  • 1 tsp Paprika
  • 1 tsp Kosher salt
  • 1 tsp Onion powder
  • 1 tsp Garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp Ground cumin
  • 1/4 tsp Cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)

Pizza Toppings

  • 1 lb Ground beef
  • 1 cup Refried beans
  • 3/4 cup Salsa (divided)
  • 1 1/2 cups Cheddar cheese (shredded)
  • 1 cup Iceberg lettuce (chopped, optional)
  • 1 cup Tomatoes (diced, optional)
  • 1/4 cup Black olives (sliced, optional)
  • 1/4 cup Green onions (sliced, optional)
  • 1/2 cup Tortilla chips (crushed, optional)
  • Sour cream and taco sauce for drizzling

How to Make Taco Pizza Step by Step

Pizza Dough

1. Pour the water into a measuring cup, add the yeast, and leave it until it's bloomed and formed a creamy frothy head, about 10 minutes.

2. Add the flour, salt, and olive oil to a bowl and pour in the yeast.

3. Mix until it comes together into a dough, about 5 minutes.

4. Dust a large surface with flour and turn the dough onto it.

5. Knead the dough by pressing the heels of your hands into the surface, pushing it away, then folding it and pulling it back. Rotate each time you knead. Continue until the dough is smooth, about 5 minutes.

6. Cut the dough into 4 pieces using a bench scraper, wrap each piece in a separate ziploc bag, and place it in the fridge.

7. Leave the dough in the fridge for 36 hours.

8. Remove the dough from the fridge 2 hours before you plan to use it.

9. Roll it into a tight ball and place it on a floured surface. Flour the top of the balls, cover with cellophane, then a tea towel, and put in a warm place.

Taco Seasoning & Beef:

1. In a small bowl, combine all taco seasoning ingredients.

2. In a skillet over medium heat, cook the ground beef until browned. Drain any excess fat.



3. Add the taco seasoning to the beef and stir until well-coated. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, then remove from heat.

Assembling the Taco Pizza:

1. Preheat your oven to 500°F (260°C).

2. Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness and place it on a pizza stone or baking sheet.

3. Spread the refried beans over the pizza dough, followed by half of the salsa.

4. Sprinkle the seasoned ground beef evenly over the beans and salsa.

5. Top with shredded cheddar cheese.

6. Bake in the preheated oven for about 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.

7. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool slightly. Top with chopped lettuce, tomatoes, olives, green onions, and crushed tortilla chips.

8. Drizzle with the remaining salsa and sour cream.

Notes:

This recipe makes one large pizza, which can serve 3-4 people. Customize the toppings to your preference. For a spicier kick, add extra cayenne pepper to the taco seasoning or serve with hot sauce.
