Chicken Devilled

Chicken Devel is a spicy and tangy Sri Lankan dish made by stir-frying marinated chicken with onions, bell peppers, tomatoes, and a blend of fiery spices. The chicken is often cooked until crispy, and the dish is finished with a splash of vinegar or lemon juice, giving it a distinctive, zesty flavor. Chicken Devel is known for its bold taste and is typically enjoyed with rice or as a flavorful appetizer. It's a popular choice in Sri Lankan cuisine for those who love intense, savory dishes with a kick.

Ingredients

  • 500g-750g of medium-sized chicken pieces (use chicken breast)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to season
  • 1/4 cup oil
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 2 large onions, cut into wedges
  • 3 medium-sized tomatoes, cut into wedges
  • 4 capsicums (banana peppers), cut into slices (substitute with bell peppers)
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 4 tablespoons tomato sauce
  • 1 teaspoon red chili flakes (increase if you need more heat)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (only if you need it to be slightly sweet for the kids)

How to Make Chicken Devel Step by Step

  1. Cut the chicken to the required size and place it in a bowl.

    Place chicken in a bowl, add turmeric (1 tsp), chili powder (1 tsp), salt (1/2 teaspoon), and combine. Set aside for 15 minutes.


  2. Place a shallow skillet or pan over medium heat, and pour 1/4 cup oil into the pan.

    Let the oil heat slightly and shallow fry the chicken until they turn golden brown on the edges.


  3. Make sure not to burn yourself while frying the chicken. Toss the chicken constantly so they fry evenly. This should take 10-15 minutes.

  4. Place the same pan with the remaining oil over medium heat.

    Add the minced garlic and ginger (1 tbsp each) to the oil and saut√© for two minutes.

    Add the onions (2 medium), capsicum (4), and tomatoes (2) to the pan.


  5. Combine and cook over medium heat for 5-7 minutes over low fire. Once the onion and capsicum turn soft with the sauce mixture, add the chicken to the pan. Cook for another 2 minutes.


  6. Cook until the sauce is thick and completely coats the chicken with a little gravy left in the bottom of the pan.

Notes:

This recipe yields a dish that typically serves 2-3 people.
The portion size may vary depending on whether it is served as a main dish or alongside other items.
