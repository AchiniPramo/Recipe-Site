Spicy Sausage Pizza

Spicy Sausage Pizza is a fiery and flavorful dish perfect for those who love a bit of heat. This pizza combines the richness of mozzarella cheese, the zest of spicy sausage, and the tanginess of tomato sauce, all on a crispy pizza crust. The spiciness of the sausage is balanced by the savory flavors of the cheese and the freshness of the vegetables, making it a crowd-pleaser.

This Spicy Sausage Pizza is savory, salty, and delicious. The crust is chewy and soft, while the marinara is bright and fresh and the mozzarella and sausage mixture are deeply flavorful. I promise you that once you try making this pizza it will become your new go-to recipe.

Ingredients

Pizza Dough

  • 2 tsp Active dry yeast
  • 4 1/2 cups 00 Flour
  • 2 tsp Kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp Extra virgin olive oil
  • All-purpose flour for dusting
  • 1 3/4 cups Water

Marinara Sauce

  • 1/4 cup Extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp Kosher salt
  • 1 bottle Passata
  • 3 Basil leaves

Spicy Sausage

  • 2 Spicy Italian sausages removed from their casing
  • 1 Sundried tomato minced
  • 1 clove Garlic minced

Spicy Sausage Pizza

  • 1/2 cup Mozzarella (shredded)

How to Make Spicy Sausage Pizza Step by Step

Pizza Dough

1. Pour the water into a measuring cup, add the yeast, and leave it until it's bloomed and formed a creamy frothy head, about 10 minutes.

2. Add the flour, salt, and olive oil to a bowl and pour in the yeast.

3. Mix until it comes together into a dough, about 5 minutes. It will not be smooth.

4. Dust a large surface with flour and turn the dough onto it.

5. Knead the dough by pressing the heels of your hands into the surface, pushing it away, then folding it and pulling it back. Rotate each time you knead. Continue until the dough is smooth, 5 minutes.

6. Cut the dough into 4 pieces using the bench scraper, wrap each piece in a separate Ziploc bag, and place it in the fridge.

7. Leave the dough in the fridge for 36 hours.

8. Remove the dough from the fridge 2 hours before you plan to use it.

9. Roll it into a tight ball and place it on a floured surface. Flour the top of the balls, cover with cellophane and then a tea towel, and put in a warm place.

Marinara Sauce:

  1. Add 1/4 cup of olive oil to a heavy-bottomed pan.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook a few minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add the passata and kosher salt and cook for 10 minutes on medium low.
  4. Add the torn basil and remove from the heat.

Spicy Sausage Mixture:

1. On medium heat, cook the sausage while breaking it up.

2. Add the minced garlic and sundried tomato and cook for 10 minutes until slightly browned.

Assembling Spicy Sausage Pizza:

1. Preheat your pizza oven to 700°F or oven to 500°F.

2. If you're using an oven and have a pizza peel, place it in the oven now.

3. Place your pizza peel into the fridge.

4. To form the pizza, press down with your fingers into the center of the dough ball.

5. Flip and do the same thing on the other side.

6. Gently pass the dough back and forth about 6 times between the palms of your hands, letting it stretch as you do so.

7. Place the dough back onto the board and using your fingers again stretch the dough by pressing from the center outwards, leaving an edge to form the crust.

8. Place a few tablespoons of marinara into the center of your dough ball and spread.

9. Sprinkle the cheese onto the marinara.

10. Add the spicy sausage mixture.

11. Place your pizza peel onto the work surface and gently pull your pizza onto it.

12. Gently stretch the pizza by pulling it from underneath.

13. Add more cheese as desired.

14. Place your pizza in the oven and turn as soon as the dough firms. Bake for 1 minute in a pizza oven or 7 minutes in a regular oven.

Notes:

Assumes one full pizza serves 2 people.
Pizzas may serve 1-2 people depending on if other side dishes are included.
